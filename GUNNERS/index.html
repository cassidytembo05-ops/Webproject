Our Story

Written in Red & White

1886

The Birth of a Giant

Founded by munitions workers at the Royal Arsenal in Woolwich as Dial Square FC. The red kit? Donated by Nottingham Forest. The legacy? Ours alone.

1913

Highbury Opens

Arsenal moved north of the river to Highbury, a decision that sparked the greatest rivalry in London football. Spurs never recovered emotionally.

1930s

The Herbert Chapman Era

Manager Herbert Chapman turned Arsenal into the most dominant club in English football, winning five league titles in eight years. The WM formation changed football forever.

1971

The First Double

Arsenal won both the First Division and FA Cup in the same season — a feat only achieved twice before. Ray Kennedy's title-winning goal at Tottenham. Chef's kiss.

1989

Anfield. 91st Minute. Thomas.

Michael Thomas scored in injury time at Anfield to snatch the title from Liverpool on goal difference. The most dramatic finale in English football history. Full stop.

2003–04
THE INVINCIBLES

Unbeaten. Unbothered.

49 league games unbeaten. 38 that season — no losses. Wenger's greatest masterpiece. A record that will stand forever because no one has the audacity to try it again.

2006

The Emirates Era Begins

Moved from beloved Highbury to the Emirates Stadium. 60,704 seats. A cathedral of football that Thierry Henry blessed with his statue outside.

NOW

The Arteta Revolution

Mikel Arteta building something special. Young, hungry, relentless. The title race is heating up and Arsenal are right in it. The wait may nearly be over. May. Nearly.
